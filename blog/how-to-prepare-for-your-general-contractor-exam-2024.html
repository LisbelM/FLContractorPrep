How to Prepare for Your General Contractor Exam in 2024

Jan 15, 2024 8 min read

Passing the Florida General Contractor exam is about consistency, not cramming. A smart plan gives you daily wins and builds confidence before test day.

Build a realistic study plan

Start by mapping out the exam topics and dividing them into weekly blocks. Aim for 30 to 60 minutes per day and increase intensity only after your routine is solid.

Use short, modular lessons

Short lessons keep you focused and reduce burnout. Pair each lesson with a handful of practice questions to lock in what you just learned.

Practice under real conditions

Schedule timed practice tests every week. Review wrong answers right away and track which topics cost you the most points.

Track and target weak areas

Progress tracking helps you study with purpose. Spend extra time on weak topics and keep your stronger areas warm with quick reviews.

Finish with full-length simulations

In the final two weeks, take full practice exams to build speed and accuracy. This is where most candidates gain the final confidence they need.
